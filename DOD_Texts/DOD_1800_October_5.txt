October 5, 1800

Admitted James McGroty. This artful chap gave his Surname to A. Musgrave as McTrosty, no doubt with intent to avoid a punishment which he anticipated, not only on account of his elopement on the 23rd August last but an aggravation of his fault by coming the next day after, tantalising his Ward mates by holding a bottle of Rum and shaking and putting it to his Mouth by way of contempt and defiance to W. Cummings who at the juncture was standing at the Corner of the fence, looking at him; in addition to all this, he had come in again with a twofold addition to his former Complaint for which see Occurences of the 16th last June.
